Core Teaching Practice #5

Implementing Norms and Routines for Classroom Discourse

When it comes to creating a classroom with effective discourse, it is important to make sure that every student feels comfortable and supported. For this artifact, I used two examples from our daily agenda. As students enter my classroom, they are expected to sit quietly and work on the notebook question. After about 2-3 minutes, we have a class discussion every single day as to what they believe the answer might be. This is a routine that students are able to look forward to every day; however, when it comes to making sure that students feel comfortable with who they are and comfortable around their classmates, students need to get to know each other better and to make connections. Every Monday, as seen above, I ask my students something along the lines of what they did over the weekend or during their time off. On Wednesdays, we have wellness Wednesday, so I like to start off class by telling them to share something good that is happening in their lives or something that they have heard. Doing both of these things has contributed to a strong class culture that respects each other and is used to discussing topics together, which leads to a much more productive class.
