Core Teaching Practice #4

Diagnosing Particular Common Patterns of Student Thinking and Development in a Subject-Matter Domain

For this artifact, I was having students write about half a page on whether they would rather live in a republic or a democracy. We were exploring this during our ancient Rome Unit, and I provided students with the video above to assist them in their decision. The video covers the main similarities and differences between a republic and a democracy. After the video, as a class, we created a Venn diagram to compare and contrast the two forms of government. After, the students began their half-page paper on which they would rather live. As I collected the papers at the end of the hour, I noticed that many students in my first hour were deciding to live in each form of government because they thought it meant being either a republican or a democrat. To combat this in future lessons, I made sure to repeat multiple times, and I wrote on the board, that living in a republic and living in a democracy do not make you either a republican or a democrat.
